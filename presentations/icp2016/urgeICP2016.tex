\frame {                      % SLIDE

    \frametitle{What is the urgency authority?}


The executive can \textbf{interfere with legislative scheduling} at will \\
(Carey\&Shugart 1998, Morgenstern 2002)

\bigskip \pause

When a bill declared \alert{urgent}:

\bi

\item Colombia: it takes precedence over other legislative business immediately

\item Brazil: assembly must act in 45 days, else it takes precedence\\ Since 2001, all \emph{medidas provis\'orias} are urgent

\item Mexico: must be scheduled for floor vote within 30 days \\ (but 4 bills/year only)

\item Chile: chamber must act in 30 days or less

\item Uruguay: must act in a pre-specified, short period, \\ inaction turns bill into law

\ei
}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\frame {                      % SLIDE

    \frametitle{Urgency authority in Chilean constitution and law}

\bi
\item Any bill at any stage can be declared urgent

\item Chamber urged must ``discuss and vote'' before 30 days

\item Law sets the breadth of the interference

\be
\item act now (\emph{discusión inmediata}, 6 days)
\item two weeks notice (\emph{urgencia suma}, 15 days)
\item four weeks notice (\emph{urgencia simple}, 30 days) 
\ee

\item Can retire the urgency, with immediate effects

\item Non-compliance: reversionary schedule/policy indetermined
\ei
}

\frame {                      % SLIDE

    \frametitle{Does it matter?}

``... can have \textcolor{green}{dramatic effects} on ex.-leg.\ relations,  \\
legislative organization, and policy...''  \\ (Morgenstern 2002) 

\bigskip

Or perhaps \alert{irrelevant} in the face of opposition: \\ schedule now $\neq$ passage

\bigskip \pause

Under-investigated (Chile only?):

\bi

\item  60\% exec.~proposals 1990--94 urgent (Siavelis 2002)

\item Speed of consideration: 
urgent 29.0 weeks~~~~~rest 29.7

\item Strongly associated with likelihood of passage \\ (Alemán\&Navia 2009)

\item Methodological obstacles: right censoring, selection bias...

\ei
